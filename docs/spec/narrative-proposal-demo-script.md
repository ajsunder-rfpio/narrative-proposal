# Narrative Proposal: Demo Script

The AE story, end to end, for walking prospective customers through the product. Each beat names the surface it happens on and the line to land. Target runtime: 12 to 15 minutes. The premise stays constant; swap the customer name and vertical to match the audience.

## Premise (30 seconds)

"Meet Dana, an account executive. She finished a discovery call with a logistics prospect yesterday afternoon. She needs a tailored proposal in front of them by Friday, and there's no proposal team to hand it to. Watch what she does with the next hour."

## Beat 1: Intake (2 min) — Intake screen

**Show:** Create the pursuit, upload the call transcript, pull the CRM record. Extraction runs. The context fields fill in: customer, problem, scope, budget signals, stakeholders, a competitor mention.

**Point at two things.** Every extracted fact has a source chip; click one and it jumps to the exact moment in the transcript. And the field the call never covered says "not found," sitting empty for Dana to fill.

**Line to land:** "It read the call. And where it doesn't know, it says so. This system never guesses about your customer, and that rule holds all the way through what you're about to see."

## Beat 2: Outline (1.5 min) — Outline view

**Show:** The annotated outline, each section carrying a purpose note tied to what the prospect actually said. Rename one section, drag another, to show Dana owns the structure.

**Line to land:** "Ten minutes ago this was a phone call. Now it's a proposal skeleton built around their problem, not our template."

## Beat 3: Win themes (2 min) — Theme workspace

**Show:** AI-drafted themes and discriminators, including a ghosting angle built from the competitor mention in the transcript. Edit one theme. Lock the set.

**Line to land:** "This is the strategy layer. Once Dana locks these, every single section the AI drafts is conditioned on them. One story, start to finish, no matter how many hands touch the document."

## Beat 4: Drafting (4 min) — Drafting canvas

The longest beat, and the one that closes deals. Three moments, in order.

**Moment 1, the draft.** Draft one section. Prose appears, written to the outline node and the locked themes.

**Moment 2, the citation.** Click a claim in the draft. The citation opens: the case study it came from, the exact passage, from the customer's own governed library. "That performance claim isn't the model's opinion. It's your approved content, and here's the receipt."

**Moment 3, the gap.** Scroll to the amber marker: "[no supporting evidence found in library]." Pause on it. "Every other tool would have written a smooth sentence right here. Ours tells you the library has no proof point for this, and gives you a search to go find one or the choice to rewrite. Fluent fiction about your own past performance is how bids die. We made it structurally impossible."

Then one inline action (tighten a paragraph) to show preview-before-apply, and note the edited claim flipping to "re-check."

## Beat 5: The evaluator (3 min) — Review view

**Show:** Run evaluation. The report comes back: an unsupported claim, a repeated paragraph across two sections, one section where a locked theme never showed up. Click "Go to fix" on the theme gap and land in the exact section.

**Line to land:** "Before a human reviewer spends a minute on this, the draft has already been scored the way an evaluator would score it. Nobody else in this market simulates the reader."

## Beat 6: Export and the loop (2 min) — Assembly and export

**Show:** Generate the executive summary from the finished document. The pre-export card: sections, pages, 34 of 35 claims verified, one warning listed with a link. Fix it or export anyway; either way the choice is Dana's and it's recorded. Export to docx.

Then the part most demos skip: the outcome prompt. Mark the pursuit won. The winning sections queue for the library.

**Line to land:** "That win just made the next proposal better. Every pursuit feeds the library, so retrieval compounds on your own history. A competitor can copy our screens. They can't copy your last two years of wins."

## Close (30 seconds)

"Dana started with a call transcript. Under an hour later she's got a cited, evaluated, tailored proposal, and her company's library got smarter. That's the product."

## Fallback notes

- If asked about Word: "Writers who live in Word will work through an add-in that keeps citations and compliance bindings intact; that ships right after this. The point is the intelligence lives in the proposal state, so the editor is a choice, and Copilot can't see any of it."
- If asked about federal: "Same engine. Solicitation parsing, the live compliance matrix, and color-team review are the fast-follow; the data model underneath already supports them."
- If evaluation runs long in a live demo, have a pre-run report ready to open.
